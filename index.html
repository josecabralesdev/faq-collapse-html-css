Frequently Asked Questions

What is a credit card and how does it work?

A credit card is a plastic card that allows you to borrow money from a financial institution to make purchases. You're given a credit limit, which is the maximum amount you can borrow. You can then use the card to make purchases and pay back the borrowed amount later, usually with interest.

Why should I get a credit card?

There are many benefits to having a credit card:
  • Building Credit: Using a credit card responsibly can help you build a good credit history, which is important for obtaining loans, mortgages, and other financial products in the future.
  • Rewards: Many credits cards offer rewards programs, such as cash back, travel points, or discounts on purchases.
  • Convenience: Credit cards are a convenient way to make purchases, both online and in person.
  • Emergency Founds: A credit card can act as a safety net in case of unexpected expenses.

What factors should I consider when choosing a credit card?

When choosing a credit card, it's important to consider these factors:
  • APR (Annual Percentage Rate): The interest rate charged on your credit card balance. A lower APR is better.
  • Annual Fee: Some cards have annual fees. Choose a card with a low or no annual fee.
  • Rewards Program: Consider what type of rewards program is most valuable to you.
  • Credit Limit: Choose a card with a credit limit that suits your needs.
  • Peaks and Benefits: Some cards offer additional perks, such as travel insurance or purchase protection.

What is a credit score and how does it affect my credit card options?

A credit card score is a numerical representation of your creditworthiness. A higher credit score generally means you have a good credit history and are more likely to approved for credit cards with better terms, such as lower APRs and higher credit limits.
